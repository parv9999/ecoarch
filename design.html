How Should We Design?

Designing a Green Roof for Urban Heat Island Mitigation

  • Structural Support:

    Load-Bearing Capacity: Ensure the building's structure can support the additional weight of the soil, plants, and water. Green roofs can be quite heavy, especially when saturated with water, so it’s essential to consult with a structural engineer.

      • ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  • Plant Selection:

    Native and Drought-Resistant Plants: Choose plants that are native to the area and can withstand local climate conditions. Drought-resistant plants reduce the need for irrigation, conserving water and lowering maintenance costs.

      • ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  • Water Management:

    Irrigation System: Install an efficient irrigation system, such as drip irrigation, to ensure plants receive adequate water, especially during dry periods.

      • ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  • Integration with Building Design:

    Ensure safe access for maintenance and for people to enjoy the green spaces, if applicable, and design the green roof to complement the building’s architecture and the surrounding urban landscape.
